CODE OF ETHICS

The Code of Ethics (CoE) stands as a guide on how to behave ethically, both within the Foundation and outside. The CoE was devised by the Ethics Committee based on experience and common sense, and was approved by the O5 Council on July 19, 1923. The CoE is expected to be followed by all Foundation personnel regardless of position. Failure to comply is punishable; the punishments are listed below in the Offense System (OFS).

The codex (link below) serves as The Law of The Foundation. The Codex must be followed at all times by all personnel. Anyone caught breaking the Codex will face trial with the Tribunal Department, even including violations of the Code of Ethics.

The Code of Ethics is one of the most important Foundation documents. It outlines the conditions of the Ethics Committee as well as all relevant humanitarian rights and power establishments regarding D-Class Personnel and the safety of Foundation Personnel. Responsibility for amending this document is ultimately reserved only for members of the Ethics Committee, and may be granted through emergency powers publicly from the Board of Directors through the public, to which the document is always granted, or to be published by the Ethics Editor(+)

The Code of Ethics stands higher than any Foundation personnel, including the O5 and The Administrator - [if highly needed]. The Code of Ethics must be enforced, no matter what their rank or site. They should have no abusive council or an unethical site.

The O5 Council is a powerful council, but the Ethics Committee is the group that oversees the O5 Council and ensures it is on the right path. It determines if high-ranking personnel commit unethical actions for punishment.

All directors of a department or leadership/department supervisors [TFS-TFL] must enforce the CoE in their own department. If they fail to do so, they may be removed by Ethics or given a Class-A through D offense. For a director to be demoted for failing to enforce the CoE, it must be reviewed by a Vice Editor, Editor, and/or Overseer.

Build-up of Offenses

Offenses can build up. This is to prevent personnel from continuously breaking the CoE and getting away with only minor punishment. The buildup is as follows:

  • If personnel receive three Class A offenses, they will be subject to a Class B offense.
  • If personnel receive three Class B offenses, they will be subject to a Class C offense.
  • If personnel receive two Class C offenses, they will be subject to a Class D offense.
  • If personnel receive two Class D offenses, they will be subject to a Class E offense.
  • If personnel receive two Class E offenses, they will be subject to a Class X offense.

In SCP terminology, "Phantom" is typically used as a cover designation or codename for a highly classified strike team or black-ops unit within the Foundation.

Because the Foundation compartmentalizes information, the real name and activities of some teams are redacted or concealed, and they are referred to with placeholder terms like "Phantom," "Specter," "Null," etc. This lets the Foundation discuss the existence of the unit without disclosing what it actually does.

Why it exists

A Phantom team is usually created for one of the following reasons:

Purpose Description
Extreme secrecy The team handles missions too sensitive, dangerous, or ethically questionable to be logged under normal task force parameters.
Anomalous operations They may use or interact with anomalies in ways that are not sanctioned for normal Foundation units.
Off-the-books actions The team may perform unauthorized containment, neutralization, or intelligence gathering that can't be traced back to the Foundation.
Internal contingency Sometimes Phantom teams exist to monitor or eliminate other Foundation personnel should they become compromised.

What "Phantom" means in-universe

The term does not refer to a specific SCP.

It's more like a codename for a hidden unit.

Their members, mission reports, and clearance are all classified.

A - CoE 0:1 - Ethics Hierarchy

Note: this is only for ethical actions (i.e., what is ethical and what is not; this is not for ordering around subjects).

The hierarchy goes as follows:

Class-A Personnel - <Executive>

Class-A Personnel >> Intelligence Personnel, Executives, O5s, and The Administrator.

Classifications are assigned to personnel based on their proximity to potentially dangerous anomalous objects, entities, or phenomena.

Class A personnel are those deemed essential to Foundation strategic operations and are not allowed direct access to anomalies under any circumstances. When circumstances require Class A personnel to be in direct proximity to such anomalies (such as in the case of facilities housing containment units), Class A personnel are not allowed access to the areas of the facility containing such anomalies and are to be kept in secured areas at all times. In the case of an emergency, Class A personnel are to be immediately evacuated to a designated and secure off-site location. O5 Council members are always Class A personnel.

Class-B Personnel - <Civil>

Class-B Personnel >> Administrative Department, Internal Tribunal Department, Ethics Committee

Class B personnel are those deemed essential to local Foundation operations and may only be granted access to objects, entities, and anomalies that have passed quarantine and have been cleared of any potential mind-affecting effects or memetic agents. In the event of a containment breach or hostile action against a Foundation facility, Class B personnel are to be evacuated to a designated, secure off-site location as soon as possible.

Class-C Personnel - <Combative Division>

Class-C Personnel >> Combative Personnel, Combative High Ranks

Class C personnel are personnel with direct access to most anomalies not deemed strictly hostile or dangerous. Class C personnel that have had direct contact with potentially mind-affecting or memetic properties may be subject to mandatory quarantine and psychiatric evaluation as deemed necessary by security personnel. In the event of a containment breach or hostile action against a Foundation facility, non-combatant Class C personnel are to either report to secure lockdown areas or be evacuated at the discretion of on-site security personnel in the case of a site-wide breach or other catastrophic event.

Class-D Personnel - <Foundation Personnel>

Class-D Personnel >> Classification-Disposable, D-Class, Security Department, Scientific Department, Medical Department, Manufacturing Department, Engineering and Technical

Class D is a classification given to those deemed non-essential to any of the Foundation's operations and henceforth were deemed "Disposable Personnel." They have access to be around any anomalies for research or security purposes, as the Foundation losing them is deemed expendable. In the case of a breach, they are to be escorted to a breach shelter by security personnel. Amnestic treatment is recommended for all site personnel and is absolutely required for any combative personnel below level 4 after a containment breach. (Note: This excludes any and all research personnel unless they saw something they shouldn't have.)

Class-E Personnel - <No Rights Entitled>

Class-E Personnel >> Classification-Epilepsy, E-Class

Class E is a provisional classification applied to field agents and containment personnel that have been exposed to potentially dangerous effects during the course of securing and establishing initial containment over a newly-designated anomalous object, entity, or phenomenon. Class E personnel are to be quarantined as soon as possible, monitored, and screened for potentially harmful changes in behavior, personality, or physiology, and may only return to duty after being fully debriefed and cleared by psychiatric and medical staff.

L - CoE 11:2 - CD Pushing Another CD Over the Line

It is unethical to terminate a Class-D personnel that was pushed across the CDC red line by another Class-D personnel. The Class-D Personnel that did the pushing is authorized for termination.

L - CoE 11:3 - Overseer Council Responsibility

The heads of departments are responsible for enforcing the Code of Ethics and punishments enacted by the Ethics Committee. THIS ALSO INCLUDES DIRECTORS, DEPUTY DIRECTORS, AND SUPERVISORS.

L - CoE 11:4 - Entering Containment Chambers

No Foundation Personnel should ever enter a containment chamber unless permitted by the Overseer of Scientific or if they are combative personnel.

L - CoE 11:5 - Interaction with SCPs as A-Class Personnel

If any Overseers interact with SCPs, it will be a Class-E through X offense. Any other A-Class Personnel interacting with SCPs will be a Class-D through Class-X offense.

L - CoE 11:6 - Breaching SCPs From Containment

Foundation Personnel breaching SCPs from their containment cell will result in the following:

  • 1 SCP: Kick / Class-C Offense
  • 2 SCPs: Class-D Offense
  • More Than 3 SCPs: Class-E Offense

Unintentional/Accidental Breaching of these SCP classes is as follows. Please note that intentionally breaching SCPs from containment will result in more severe punishment.

  • Breaching a Safe Class SCP is a Class-B Offense.
  • Breaching a Euclid Class SCP is a Class-C Offense.
  • Breaching a Keter Class SCP is a Class-E Offense.

L - CoE 11:7 - Ethical Testing of Disposable Personnel

For a test to be ethical, the Disposable Personnel must be briefed on what they will be tested on and why. Research personnel must pick a reasonable amount of Disposable Personnel for their test. A test has officially begun when a Class-D enters the containment chamber. The Ethics Committee may cease a test if they feel it is not ethical for statistical analysis.

L - CoE 11:8 - Ethical Testing

ONLY THE RESEARCH DEPARTMENT MAY TEST DISPOSABLE PERSONNEL. Any Non-Researching Personnel (any other personnel not in SRADD) caught testing Disposable Personnel will be ceased immediately and will be given an adequate punishment depending on if the CD is harmed. If the CD is harmed during this event, the personnel will be terminated or given a demotion; if the CD is safe and healthy, the personnel will be given a Class-C offense.

L - CoE 11:9 - Exiting The Facility And Entering Facility Grounds

Only Mobile Task Force Units of NU-7, Epsilon-11, and Alpha-1 are granted exit. The Intelligence Agency is also granted clearance to exit Gate A. Any non-cleared personnel may not exit the Facility unless given permission by an Overseer. Failure to abide by this article and exiting will result in lethal termination.

Any non-cleared personnel entering the facility will be lethally terminated on the consensus of the Ethics Committee. The personnel allowed to enter are as follows:

  • Groups of Interest (PERMISSION REQUIRED)
  • Mobile Task Force Units Of: Nu-7, Alpha-1, R-1, Epsilon-11, Beta-7, or Omega-1
  • Intelligence Agency
  • Any Transferee Personnel
  • Any personnel with permission from an Overseer who also bears Security Clearance 5
  • Any FTOs, FTOSs, FTO/As, or other personnel authorized to train if accompanied by Combative Personnel.

Foundation Personnel are not allowed in certain zones unless they bear specific security clearances. The list of sectors and required clearances is as follows:

If procedures are not followed during these events, the assigned personnel to retrieve said personnel will be terminated.

L - CoE 11:11 - Ethics O5 Representative (O5-6)

The O5 Representative to the Ethics Committee represents the Council within the Ethics Committee. They advise the Committee and the Board of Directors and help set an overall direction for the Committee.

The O5 Representative cannot remove members of the Ethics Committee and cannot enact laws or change the Code of Ethics without the approval of the Committee, unless by executive order. It is important to remember that the O5 Representative is not an Overseer. They do not rule over the Committee like many other O5 Councilors do with their departments. The O5 Representative may not vote unless a tie has occurred. The Ethics Committee may not remove the O5 Representative unless he/she massively breaks the CoE.

Being immature or unprofessional is strictly prohibited. This includes but is not limited to mentioning skibidi/skibidi toilet, 6 7, rizz, or any other immature term/Gen Z term.

Using surgical tools given to you to do your job to murder Class-Ds without proper authority is strictly against the Code of Ethics and will result in you being relieved of your duties as medical personnel.

To properly give any medical treatment to a CD, you must be accompanied by 2 SDs to the CDC and into the cellzone to pick up the D-Class.

To properly handle a Group of Interest in the Foundation, you must try to make as much peace as you can with this Group of Interest. Failure to do so may put the Foundation under more stress than it already is.

Any Non-D.E.A. Making the Foundation not neutral or an enemy to any Group of Interest for no apparent reason is strictly prohibited and will result in demotion.

[SECTION-14] UNETHICAL OPERATIONS

Unethical Foundation Operations are operations carried out by Foundation Personnel either unauthorized or unethical. Therefore, it is tasked by the Ethics Committee to ensure that all Foundation Operations are ethically well-made and still accomplish the objective.

O - CoE 14:0 - Unethical Foundation Operational Activities

Unethical Foundation Operations consist of personnel doing the following:

  • Carrying out unauthorized operations
  • Doing things inside of an operation not previously authorized and not necessary to save life or property
  • Violating the Foundation's regulations, guidelines, or ethical standards while participating in an operation

O - CoE 14:1 - Ethical Standards of Foundation Operations

Any Foundation operations must be approved by the Overseer Council. They must follow the ethical guidelines and regulations of the Foundation. If an operation fails to meet these standards, then the Ethics Committee may cease the operation.

[SECTION-15] ETHICAL JUSTICE

Ethical Justice means that justice must be done efficiently and swiftly without any corruption by the Internal Tribunal Department.

P - CoE 15:0 - How Trial Works

  1. The Internal Tribunal Department may take you to trial as you may have violated the Code of Ethics.
  2. If you violated this in trial, you MUST HAVE A LAWYER or it may be considered unfair.
  3. If you do not have a lawyer, one will be appointed to you by the Foundation.
  4. If there is no clip or screenshot evidence, you must have 3+ witnesses; otherwise, your claim or defense will be dismissed.
  5. If the judge gives you an unfair punishment, you can report it to an Ethics Senior Member+ and they will take action against it (with evidence of your conviction).

P - CoE 15:1 - Unethical Detainment During Trial

Detaining anyone during a trial is strictly prohibited unless they prove a threat towards anyone there.

P - CoE 15:2 - Unethical Termination During Trial

Terminating anyone during trial requires that they are armed and/or too dangerous. If this article is broken, it will be punished accordingly.
